Section 8.4 - More Series Tests!:

In this section, we’ll learn tests that can be applied to series that may have some negative terms.

Alternating Series Test (AST):

If the alternating series
 
∑ n=1∞(−1)n−1b n = b1 − b2 + b3 − b4 + b5 − b6 + …,bn > 0
 
 
satisﬁes both of the conditions listed below, then the series is convergent.

(i)
bn+1 ≤ bn
(ii)
limn→∞bn = 0

Alternating Series Estimation Theorem:

If S = ∑ n=1∞(−1)n−1b n is the sum of a convergent alternating series, then

|Rn| = |S − Sn|≤ bn+1

Absolute & Conditional Convergence:

A series ∑ an is called absolutely convergent if the series ∑ |an| is convergent.
 

A series ∑ an is called conditionally convergent if it is convergent, but not absolutely convergent.

Theorem:
If a series ∑ an is absolutely convergent, then it is convergent.
